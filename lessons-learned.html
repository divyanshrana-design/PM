Lessons Learned Log

Run this session within a week of closing the project. 30 minutes with the core team. Use the Keep, Stop, Start, Try framework. Keep answers honest, specific and short.

Project: [Project name] Close date: [dd/mm/yyyy] Facilitator: [Your name] Attendees: [Names]

1. Outcome summary

Dimension Plan Actual Variance / reason
End date [15 Dec] [18 Dec] [3 day slip, venue contract delay]
Budget [€2,255] [€2,410] [€155 over, extra printing]
Main goal [Raise €15,000] [€16,420 raised] [Exceeded by 9%]
Scope [As per charter] [Added raffle element] [Minor scope addition, handled without impact]

2. Keep (what worked, do it again)

  • [Weekly 15 minute team standups kept everyone aligned without wasting time]
  • [Using Buffer to schedule a month of social posts in one session]
  • [The two named backups per volunteer role saved us twice]

3. Stop (what did not work, drop it)

  • [Long email threads with the board, moved to a shared Google Doc mid project]
  • [Trying to design graphics in house, we lost 2 days and had to hire anyway]

4. Start (what we wish we had done)

  • [Start the donor thank you process a week earlier, people expect a quick reply]
  • [Start using an AI meeting notetaker from project kick off]
  • [Start tracking budget in Google Sheets from day one, not day thirty]

5. Try (what we want to experiment with next time)

  • [Try a second smaller spring event to test new messaging]
  • [Try running the fundraising campaign in Kanban rather than Gantt]

6. Risks that actually happened

Which risks on the project charter came true? Which did not? Any surprises that were not on the register at all?

  • [Low attendance risk did not happen, social ads saved us]
  • [Volunteer illness risk did happen, backups handled it in 20 minutes]
  • [Surprise: stripe payout delayed 5 days, not on our risk register, added for next time]
