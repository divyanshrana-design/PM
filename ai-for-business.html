The Playbook

7 ways Irish SMEs & charities are using AI this week

Each play includes the job to be done, the recommended tool, and a copy paste prompt you can adapt in 30 seconds.

01
Planning

Turn a rough idea into a full project plan

The job

You have a vague goal like "run a spring fundraiser" or "launch the new menu" and no plan. Turn it into a phased project in under 5 minutes.

Recommended tool

ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini (all have free tiers). For PM tools with AI built in: ClickUp Brain or Notion AI.

What good looks like

  • Phases + workstreams
  • Tasks with owners + deadlines
  • Top 3 risks flagged
Copy paste prompt 
Act as a senior project manager for a small Irish [business type, e.g. café / charity].
Our goal: [describe outcome in one sentence].
Our team: [e.g. 4 people, 2 part-time].
Budget: €[amount]. Deadline: [date].

Produce:
1. A 1-paragraph project charter.
2. A phased plan (Initiation, Planning, Execution, Monitoring, Closing) with 3 to 5 tasks per phase.
3. For each task: owner role, estimated effort in hours, and dependency.
4. Top 3 risks with a mitigation for each.
5. A 1-line comms plan.

Keep the language plain. Assume we are non-PM professionals.
02
Communication

Write the awkward email in 10 seconds

The job

Chasing a late invoice, declining a request, updating a funder on a delay. AI writes the first draft. You just add the facts and send.

Recommended tool

Any chat AI. Inside Outlook/Gmail, use Copilot or Gemini directly.

Rule of thumb

Never paste personal or confidential client data into free tier AI tools. See the GDPR section below.

Copy paste prompt 
Write a short, friendly but firm email in an Irish business tone.

Context: [e.g. Invoice #INV-034 for €1,200 is 21 days overdue. Client is "Shore Ltd". I've already sent one reminder.]
Goal: [e.g. get them to pay this week without damaging the relationship].
Tone: warm, professional, low pressure.
Length: under 120 words.

Offer two versions, one softer and one firmer. Leave a [YOUR NAME] placeholder.
03
Meetings

Never take meeting minutes again

The job

Meetings eat your week. AI notetakers record, transcribe, summarise, and pull out actions automatically. You leave the call with a clean list.

Recommended tool

Fathom (free, Zoom/Teams/Meet), Otter.ai, or Microsoft Copilot if you're already on Teams.

Irish context tip

Inform attendees you're recording — required under GDPR and simple common courtesy. Most tools display a notice automatically.

Post meeting prompt 
I'm pasting the transcript of a meeting below.

Give me:
1. A 5-bullet summary (non-technical language).
2. Decisions made (bullet list).
3. Action items in a table: [Owner | Action | Due date].
4. Any risks or unresolved questions.
5. A 3 line follow up email I can send to attendees.

04
Funding & Sales

Draft grant applications & proposals 4× faster

The job

For charities: grant applications that used to take a full day now take 90 minutes. For SMEs: sales proposals, tender responses, LEO grant forms.

Recommended tool

Claude (excellent long form writing, handles big documents), or ChatGPT with a custom GPT trained on your past successful applications.

Pro move

Keep a "winning proposals" folder. Feed 2–3 past winners into the AI as reference — quality jumps immediately.

Copy paste prompt 
You are a grant writing specialist for Irish [charities, social enterprises or SMEs].

I'm applying to: [funder name + programme].
Their stated priorities: [paste from their website].
Word limit per section: [e.g. 300].

About us:
- Mission: [...]
- Beneficiaries: [...]
- Last year's impact: [...]

Draft the following sections, matching the funder's language:
1. Summary of the project
2. Need / problem we're solving
3. Activities + timeline
4. Outcomes + how we'll measure them
5. Budget justification (rough)

Flag any section where I need to provide more evidence before submitting.
05
Marketing

A month of social content in one sitting

The job

Stop staring at Canva on a Sunday. Generate a month of on brand posts, captions, and hashtags — then schedule and forget.

Recommended tool

ChatGPT + Canva AI (Magic Design, Magic Write) — an unbeatable combo for a team of one. Schedule with Buffer or Later (free tiers).

Irish context tip

Feed the AI your tone of voice and a few past posts that did well. Generic AI output sounds American — specifics make it sound like you.

Copy paste prompt 
Act as a social media manager for [business name], a [type] based in [Irish town/city].

Brand voice: [e.g. warm, witty, plain-spoken, never corporate].
Audience: [e.g. local 30–50 year olds, parents, small business owners].
Goal this month: [e.g. drive footfall to new menu].

Give me 12 social posts for Instagram + Facebook, spread over 4 weeks.
For each post: hook, 2–3 sentence caption, 5 hashtags (mix local + niche), suggested image description.

Mix these post types: behind-the-scenes, customer story, product spotlight, tip, question, offer.
Write in an Irish voice — no Americanisms. Use "we" and speak to one person.
06
Admin & Ops

Kill your spreadsheet hell

The job

Turn a messy spreadsheet into clean data, summaries, or charts. Draft SOPs. Write formulas. Clean up customer lists.

Recommended tool

ChatGPT (with the free "Advanced Data Analysis" feature) or Claude. For ongoing automations: Zapier or Make.com with AI steps.

30 second demo

Upload a CSV, say "find duplicate customers and flag the 10 who haven't ordered in 6 months" — done.

Copy paste prompt 
I'm going to paste some data below.

Please:
1. Tell me what you see (rows, columns, likely meaning).
2. Flag any data quality issues (duplicates, missing fields, weird values).
3. Give me 3 useful summaries or insights for a small business owner.
4. Give me the Excel/Google Sheets formulas to get each of those summaries myself.
5. If it would help, describe a simple chart I could make.

Data:
"""
[paste here — remove any personal names/emails first]
"""
07
Strategy

Your weekly 15 minute "AI board advisor"

The job

Most small business owners have no one to think out loud with. Every Friday, spend 15 minutes briefing an AI on the week and asking it to challenge you.

Recommended tool

Claude or ChatGPT — paid tier (~€20/month) is worth it here for memory + reasoning.

Why it works

You'll catch drift early. You'll name the thing you've been avoiding. You'll leave the week with a short, honest plan.

Friday prompt 
You're my weekly board advisor for [business name], a small [type] in Ireland.

Here's this week:
- Wins: [...]
- Losses / blockers: [...]
- Numbers that moved: [...]
- What I dodged or avoided: [...]

Do this:
1. Reflect what you're hearing in 3 bullets.
2. Name the single most important thing I should do next week, and why.
3. Play devil's advocate — challenge one assumption I'm making.
4. Suggest one metric to watch for the next 4 weeks.

Be direct. I don't need to be flattered.

Do this before you paste anything

AI safely: GDPR & common sense for Irish SMEs

Never paste personal data into free tiers

Free tier AI tools may train on your inputs. Customer names, emails, health info, financial data → strip them out or use paid or enterprise tiers that contractually don't train on your data.

Check the data processing agreement

If you're on the paid tier of ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot or Gemini Business, a DPA is available. Your DPO (or accountant) will want to see it before you roll out team wide.

Always have a human in the loop

AI drafts. Humans decide. Especially for: pricing, hiring, legal, health, and anything the customer will see externally.

Write a one page AI policy

Even for a 4 person team. Cover: allowed tools, what data is off limits, who signs off on AI generated external content. Saves heartache later.

A note on the EU AI Act

The EU AI Act is phasing in through 2025–2027. For most Irish SMEs using off the shelf AI tools for admin work, the practical impact is low, but being transparent with customers when AI is involved (e.g. an AI chatbot on your site) becomes mandatory. Start being transparent now and you'll never have to scramble later.

Your first month

The 30 day AI rollout for a small team

Week 1

Play with it personally

You (the owner/lead) use ChatGPT or Claude free tier every day for one week. No team rollout yet. Just build intuition.

Week 2

Pick one workflow

Pick one repeating task that hurts, meeting notes, sales follow ups or social posts, and automate just that one. Measure time saved.

Week 3

Train the team (1 hour)

Show the team your wins. Share 3 prompts. Write the one page AI policy together. Add your 5 favourite prompts to a shared Notion or Doc.

Week 4

Decide what to pay for

Based on what's actually being used, upgrade one tool to paid tier. Review in 3 months — not before.
