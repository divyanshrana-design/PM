Prompt Library Starter

30 copy paste prompts for the jobs Irish small teams actually do every week. Each prompt is tuned to produce practical, Irish context aware output. Replace anything in [SQUARE BRACKETS] with your own info before sending.

📋

1. Project planning (6 prompts)

1.1 Turn a vague idea into a phased project plan

You are an experienced project manager for Irish SMEs. I want to run [PROJECT IDEA, e.g. "an autumn fundraising drive to raise €15,000"]. Give me a phased plan using the 5 phases of PM (initiation, planning, execution, monitoring, closing). For each phase list 3 to 5 specific tasks with rough timelines. Assume a team of 4 with a 3 month window. Use plain English, no corporate jargon.

1.2 Draft a one page project charter

Using the notes below, draft a one page project charter with these sections: problem, goal, in scope, out of scope, budget table, top 3 risks, key dates, roles, approval block. Keep it under 500 words. Notes: [PASTE YOUR NOTES]

1.3 Generate a work breakdown structure

Break the following project into a work breakdown structure with phases, deliverables and specific tasks. Include owner suggestion and rough duration for each task. Project: [DESCRIBE PROJECT]. Team size: [X people]. Deadline: [DATE].

1.4 Identify project risks

For this project: [DESCRIBE PROJECT], list the 10 most likely risks a small Irish SME or charity would face. For each risk give likelihood (1-5), impact (1-5), and one practical mitigation. Format as a table.

1.5 Build a stakeholder map

For this project: [DESCRIBE PROJECT] in Ireland, list likely stakeholders and plot each on a power vs. interest matrix. For each, recommend a communication cadence (daily, weekly, monthly, ad hoc).

1.6 Estimate a realistic budget

For a [TYPE OF PROJECT] in Ireland with [DESCRIPTION], give me a realistic budget in euros. Break it into categories, add a 10% contingency, and flag the 2 or 3 items where people typically underestimate.
✉️

2. Client and funder communications (6 prompts)

2.1 Chase a late invoice politely but firmly

Write a polite but firm email chasing [CLIENT NAME] for invoice [INVOICE NUMBER] for €[AMOUNT] which is now [X days] overdue. Offer two versions, one softer, one firmer. Irish business tone. Leave a [YOUR NAME] placeholder.

2.2 Decline a scope request without losing the client

Write a short email declining this client request: [DESCRIBE REQUEST]. Explain why it is out of scope, offer a paid change request option, keep the relationship warm. Under 150 words.

2.3 Update a funder on a delay

Write a transparent email to our funder [FUNDER] explaining that project [PROJECT NAME] will deliver [X weeks] later than planned due to [REASON]. Show what we have learned, what we are doing to recover, and ask for their input. Honest, respectful, no corporate fluff.

2.4 Draft a weekly status update

Using these notes from my week: [PASTE NOTES], write a weekly status update with RAG status (green, amber, red) for schedule, budget, scope and team, 3 to 5 bullets of what we did, 3 to 5 bullets of what we do next, blockers and decisions needed. 250 words maximum.

2.5 Write a board paper summary

Summarise this [NUMBER] page document into a one page board paper for a small Irish charity. Use these headings: context, options considered, recommendation, risks, decision required. Document: [PASTE OR ATTACH]

2.6 Reply to a complaint

Draft an empathetic, professional response to this complaint from a [CUSTOMER or SERVICE USER]. Acknowledge the issue, explain what we will do, commit to a timeline, offer a way to escalate if they are not happy. Complaint: [PASTE COMPLAINT].
🎙️

3. Meetings and notes (6 prompts)

3.1 Turn transcript into actions

Here is the transcript of our team meeting: [PASTE TRANSCRIPT]. Give me: 1) a 3 sentence summary, 2) a list of decisions made, 3) a clear action list with owner and due date for each, 4) any questions that were raised but not answered.

3.2 Draft a 30 minute agenda

Draft a tight 30 minute agenda for a meeting about [TOPIC] with [ATTENDEES]. Include time for each item, a pre read suggestion, and a decision log at the end.

3.3 Prepare for a difficult conversation

I need to have a difficult conversation with [WHO] about [TOPIC]. Give me 3 opening lines, 3 likely pushbacks and how to handle each, and one line I can use to end the conversation well regardless of outcome.

3.4 Summarise for someone who missed the meeting

Summarise these meeting notes for [PERSON WHO MISSED IT] in their role as [ROLE]. Focus only on what they need to know and what is expected of them. Max 150 words. Notes: [PASTE]

3.5 Draft follow up email after the meeting

Based on these meeting notes: [PASTE], draft a 3 line follow up email I can send to all attendees. Include: 1 line recap, 3 bullet actions with owners, one question to keep the momentum going.

3.6 Generate discussion questions

Give me 5 good discussion questions to ask our team about [TOPIC]. They should open up real conversation, not be answered yes or no, and be specific enough to lead somewhere useful.
🎯

4. Grant writing (6 prompts)

4.1 Summarise grant guidelines

Here are the guidelines for [GRANT NAME]: [PASTE]. Summarise: 1) who is eligible, 2) key dates, 3) maximum amount, 4) what they fund and do not fund, 5) any non obvious requirements. Plain English.

4.2 Draft a compelling need statement

Draft a 200 word need statement for a grant application to [FUNDER] for our project [PROJECT]. Our context: [2 or 3 sentences]. Evidence I can cite: [STATS OR SOURCES]. Warm, specific, not generic.

4.3 Build the project plan section

Using the following project brief: [PASTE], write the "project plan and methodology" section of a grant application. Include activities, outputs and outcomes in a clear logical flow. Around 500 words.

4.4 Generate monitoring and evaluation plan

Draft a simple M&E plan for this project: [DESCRIBE]. Include 4 to 6 measurable indicators, how each will be measured, how often, and who is responsible. Realistic for a small Irish charity, not PhD level.

4.5 Reuse a winning proposal

Here is a grant application we won last year: [PASTE]. We are now applying to [NEW FUNDER] for [NEW PROJECT]. Rewrite the strongest sections to fit the new context without being obviously copy pasted. Flag anything I need to update with current numbers.

4.6 Write a thank you to a funder

Write a short, sincere thank you note to [FUNDER] for awarding us [AMOUNT] for [PROJECT]. Mention one specific thing we plan to do with the money. Warm but professional. Under 100 words.

5. Marketing and social (6 prompts)

5.1 Generate a month of social posts

We are a [TYPE OF BUSINESS OR CHARITY] in [LOCATION]. Our key message this month is [MESSAGE]. Draft 20 on brand social posts for Instagram and LinkedIn. Mix of educational, behind the scenes, promotional and community. Include suggested images or visuals for each.

5.2 Turn a blog post into 10 social posts

Here is a blog post: [PASTE]. Turn it into 10 separate social posts, each standalone, with a different angle. Include at least 2 quote cards, 2 listicles, 1 question post, 1 statistic highlight. Tone: conversational, Irish.

5.3 Write a press release

Write a press release about [NEWS]. Target: Irish local and national press. Include: standard "FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE" header, 3 paragraph body, 1 quote from [NAME], boilerplate about the organisation, media contact. Active voice, 400 words maximum.

5.4 Draft a newsletter

Draft our monthly newsletter based on these updates: [PASTE]. Structure: punchy intro, 3 to 5 highlight stories (around 80 words each), a call to action, a thank you. Warm, conversational, not corporate.

5.5 Improve a piece of copy

Here is a piece of copy: [PASTE]. Rewrite it to be more direct, specific and active. Remove jargon. Keep it under [WORD COUNT] words. Tone: friendly, confident, Irish English.

5.6 Generate hashtag and caption variations

For this post: [PASTE], give me 5 caption variations (short, medium, with question, with quote, story led) and 10 hashtag combinations relevant to [INDUSTRY] in Ireland.
