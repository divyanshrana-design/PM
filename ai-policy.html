AI Acceptable Use Policy

The smallest AI policy a 4 to 20 person Irish team actually needs. GDPR aware. EU AI Act aware. Readable by everyone on the team.

Organisation: [Your organisation name] Version: 1.0 Effective from: [dd/mm/yyyy] Review date: [12 months from effective date]

1. Why we have this policy

AI tools can save our team hours every week. They can also leak confidential information, generate false claims, and put us in breach of GDPR. This policy explains what we allow, what we do not, and who to ask when in doubt.

2. Approved tools

These are the AI tools our team is approved to use for work:

ToolTierApproved uses
Microsoft 365 Copilot Paid business licence Drafting documents, summarising emails, Excel formulas
Google Gemini for Workspace Paid business licence Docs, Sheets, Gmail drafting and summaries
ChatGPT Team or Enterprise paid tier Brainstorming, draft writing, research
Claude Team paid tier Long form writing, document analysis, grant drafts
[Any other tool] [Tier] [Use case]

Any AI tool not on this list must be approved by [named approver, usually the DPO or founder] before being used with any work information.

3. Data that must never go into AI tools

  • Customer, donor or beneficiary names, emails, phone numbers or addresses
  • Financial information such as bank details, salary data or card numbers
  • Health, medical or sensitive personal data of any kind
  • Private contracts, NDA protected content, or trade secrets
  • Internal passwords, API keys or login credentials
  • Anything a client or funder has asked us to keep confidential

If you are unsure, replace names and numbers with [CLIENT A] or [AMOUNT] before pasting into the AI tool.

4. AI generated external content

Anything that leaves our organisation must be reviewed by a human before sending. That includes:

  • Emails to clients, funders or the public
  • Grant applications and funder reports
  • Social media posts, blog posts and press releases
  • Website copy and marketing materials

The reviewer is responsible for accuracy, tone and legal compliance. AI can draft. Humans approve.

5. Disclosure and transparency

Where required by the EU AI Act or by courtesy, we will disclose the use of AI:

  • Any customer facing chatbot will clearly state "this is an AI assistant"
  • Meeting recordings and AI transcription will be announced at the start of calls
  • AI generated imagery used externally will be labelled as such

6. Who is responsible for what

  • Every team member follows this policy and asks when in doubt
  • Team leads review AI drafted external content before it goes out
  • [Named DPO or equivalent] approves new AI tools and handles GDPR questions
  • [Founder or Executive Director] owns this policy and reviews it annually

7. Reporting a problem

If you think personal or confidential data has been sent to an AI tool by mistake, tell [named DPO] within 24 hours. Under GDPR, some incidents must be reported to the Data Protection Commission within 72 hours. Early reporting protects the organisation and the people affected.

8. Staff acknowledgement

By signing below you confirm you have read and understood this policy and agree to follow it in your work.
